In many countries around the world, the standard workday ends around 6 PM. However, the exact timing can vary depending on cultural norms, industry standards, and even the sector in which an individual works. Below is an overview of some countries where the traditional workday often finishes at 6 PM.

1. Germany

Germany is known for its structured work environment and a healthy work-life balance. The typical workday in Germany begins around 9 AM and ends at 6 PM, with a one-hour lunch break included. The "9 to 6" schedule is common in both public and private sectors, though some companies offer flexible working hours to promote employee well-being.

2. France

In France, the legal standard workweek is 35 hours, established in 2000 to promote work-life balance. While many workers adhere to the 9 AM to 6 PM routine, it's not uncommon to see employees leaving work at 6 PM, especially in industries such as finance, law, and tech. France also places a strong emphasis on personal time, with strict laws limiting after-hours emails and communication.

3. Spain

Despite its reputation for long lunch breaks and the traditional "siesta," Spain has gradually modernized its work schedules. In many professional sectors, especially in urban areas, the typical workday follows the 9 AM to 6 PM pattern, with a longer lunch break around 2 PM. While some businesses may still close in the afternoon and reopen later, the trend is shifting towards continuous workdays that end at 6 PM.

4. United Kingdom

The workday in the UK commonly runs from 9 AM to 5 or 6 PM. The "9 to 6" schedule is particularly common in corporate settings like finance, law, and consulting. Many employees, however, take advantage of flexible hours or remote work options, allowing them to finish earlier or later than 6 PM depending on their personal and professional needs.

5. Italy

Italy also has a strong tradition of ending the workday at 6 PM, particularly in urban areas and corporate environments. In some parts of Italy, particularly in the south, the traditional midday break (siesta) is still observed, but in modern businesses, particularly in the north, the workday is often continuous and ends at 6 PM.

6. Australia

In Australia, the workday typically starts around 9 AM and finishes at 5 or 6 PM, depending on the company and industry. Many professionals in corporate roles adhere to this schedule, while more flexible working hours have also become common in recent years, allowing employees to finish at different times.

7. Canada

In Canada, the traditional workday often ends at 5 or 6 PM, especially in office environments. This schedule is common across many sectors, including government, finance, and technology. However, flexible work arrangements and telecommuting are growing in popularity, allowing for more variation in when people finish work.

8. Japan

Japan has a reputation for long working hours, but the official workday in many companies ends at 6 PM. While it is true that many employees often stay late, particularly in more traditional or hierarchical businesses, the official working hours usually start around 9 AM and finish at 6 PM.

9. South Korea

In South Korea, the official workday in many industries runs from 9 AM to 6 PM, though overtime is common. South Korea has been working on improving work-life balance in recent years, and many companies have begun to strictly enforce a 6 PM end to the workday to reduce excessive overtime.

10. Russia

In Russia, the typical workday for most office employees starts at 9 AM and ends at 6 PM. This schedule is especially common in public administration, corporate sectors, and service industries. However, certain industries may have different schedules, with flexible hours or longer workdays.
